Automatic plant watering system

Automatic plant watering systems have made life easier for commercial bonsai growing businesses. Water must be provided to plants through irrigation in order for them to grow and develop properly. Technology development has led to the creation of numerous effective water delivery systems that operate entirely automatically and without any human input.

In this blog, we are taking into consideration, two of these automatic plant watering system which are Blumat self-watering system and Gardena Micro drip system where one uses ceramic sensor and the other uses controllers for the purpose of automation.

Blumat self-watering system

Made from the company named Blumat, this is an automatic bonsai watering system that uses a ceramic sensor named Troph (means drip in German) Blumat for its function. An irrigation tube is connected just above the valve that is forced opened or shut when the valve opens and closes.

Tropf Blumat is a self-watering system that uses sensors to monitor the moisture in the soil around it. Sensor's purpose is to automatically open or close a valve to control the amount of water that gets to the plant. Each Blumat has a completely enclosed environment inside and outside, only having access to the outside world through its pores.

Positive impact

  • It lessens water waste while promoting healthy plant development.

  • Blumat systems can be connected to a timer or a water source and can be modified to fit various plants.

  • Blumat's self-watering technology eliminates the need for manual watering of plants, which can save time and labor.

  • Plants receive the appropriate level of moisture at all times thanks to the system's automatic delivery of water as needed.

Negative impact

  • It can be expensive for people to buy and install.

  • Regular maintenance is required to keep the system operating efficiently.

  • This system must be installed by experts.



Gardena Microdrip System

An irrigation system called the Gardena Micro drip system is designed for use with small gardens and plants. Additionally, it is made up of numerous essential components including emitters, control valves, etc. The irrigation system's dripper can be selected from a number of dripper collections. These drippers are attached to the soil close to the plant using tubing. It consists of a main pipeline, controllers which control the flow of water through the pipes. The controls help irrigate the plants concurrently without any human interference or disruption.

    The common controllers used in this system are as follows:

  • The system's watering schedule can be set with timer controller. The timer can be set to turn on and off the water at particular times of day or on particular days of the week.

  • The water flow through the system is controlled by flow control valve.

  • Distribution tubing: The MicroDrip System's water distribution is controlled by this controller. It links the water supply to the emitters and can be adjusted as well.

Positive impact

  • It save human labor to supply water everyday by a human and lets automation do it for them.

  • Plants get the proper amount of moisture at the right time.

  • It can be used to water different types of Bonsai plants and hence is flexible system.

Negative impact

  • It can be expensive for people to buy and install.

  • Regular maintenance is required to keep the system operating efficiently.

  • This system must be installed by experts.

Impact of this technology while selling Bonsai plants

  • For people with busy work schedule, buying and caring for Bonsai tress could be a challenge. However, with automatic bonsai watering systems people can non- hesitantly buy bonsai trees.

  • With least water wastage and timely irrigation, the bonsai trees in the growing centers become healthy and of good quality. This fosters the business of commercial bonsai producers.

  • The production of Bonsai trees increase and cost of them becomes relatively low since there is no need of extra employees for the purpose of irrigation.

  • They can increase the sale of bonsai plants by offering a better method for watering and taking care of the plants.

Conclusion

In a nutshell, in addition to these two automated plant watering systems, there are more automated systems that water plants. They also possess a few downsides but more additional benefits. These systems can be quite successful for the production of bonsai for sale. By using these technologies, bonsai plants no longer require a person's employment to be timely watered. For the big commercial businesses, the expense and upkeep are not a significant issue or disadvantage. But employing these systems to irrigate bonsai in homes or businesses is initially expensive, however doing so eventually pays off. Since the Bonsai trees would be supplied with water timely, people could go on holidays and lengthy journeys without worrying.
